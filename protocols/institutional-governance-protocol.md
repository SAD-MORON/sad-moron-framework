# Institutional Governance Protocol

## Purpose

This protocol defines the institutional governance boundary within which SAD Moron review and decision processes are interpreted.

## Boundary

This protocol governs institutional meaning and accountable review roles.

It does not govern:
- software execution
- operational staffing models
- legal advice automation
- implementation-specific access control

## Responsibilities

This protocol is responsible for defining:
- the relationship between governance review and institutional decision
- the obligation to preserve accountable human authority
- the distinction between framework review and operational action
- the role of bounded evidence in institutional interpretation

## Exclusions

This protocol excludes:
- autonomous decision-making by tools
- silent promotion of technical findings into institutional truth
- substitution of software outputs for accountable human authority

## Enforcement Rules

### Rule 1: Human accountability remains explicit

Institutional decisions must remain attributable to responsible human authority.

### Rule 2: Governance before implementation

Implementation convenience must not override institutional governance boundaries.

### Rule 3: Review does not equal action

A governance finding, including omission-related evidence, does not by itself authorize operational action without the appropriate institutional process.

### Rule 4: Traceable responsibility

The transition from evidence review to institutional decision must remain traceable for later audit and reconstruction.

## Summary

SAD Moron governance exists to structure accountable institutional review, not to automate institutional authority away.
